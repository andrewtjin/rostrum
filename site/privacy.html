Privacy

Rostrum counts anonymous downloads; and nothing else.

Rostrum ships on two platforms, each with one install file: the Word add-in's manifest.xml and the Google Docs port's Code.gs. When you download either from this site, the matching anonymous counter on our end goes up by one. Those two counters (and their sum) are the entire extent of what Rostrum collects.

What is collected

  • Two anonymous numbers: running totals of how many times the Word manifest.xml and the Google Docs Code.gs have each been downloaded. We also publish their sum (a rough cross-platform total). None of these is tied to you, your device, or anything you do afterward.

What is not collected

  • Nothing from your documents. Rostrum never sends, stores, or sees the text of anything you write or format, in Word or in Google Docs.
  • No personal data. No names, emails, accounts, or sign-ins. There is no account to make.
  • No IP addresses or device fingerprints are retained by Rostrum. The counters record only that a download happened, never who or from where; we keep no request logs of our own. (Files are delivered through Cloudflare, which may process requests in transit under its own privacy policy.)
  • No tracking inside Word. The add-in itself sends nothing back as you use it. The count happens only at the moment you download manifest.xml from this website.
  • No tracking inside Google Docs. The Rostrum Code.gs only ever changes font size and style definitions; it sends nothing back as you use it: no usage pings, no document content. The recommended install is to make a copy of a template, which downloads nothing, so it is not counted. The only number Rostrum records is a single Code.gs download, and only if you used the counted link in the Advanced manual install, not the template copy.

Why

It tells us roughly how many people are using Rostrum over a season: enough to know the project is worth maintaining, and nothing more.

The number is deliberately approximate, and it leans in both directions depending on the platform:

  • Word over-counts a little: re-downloading manifest.xml to pick up a new version counts again.
  • Google Docs is largely uncounted by design: the recommended install is to make a copy of the template, which downloads nothing, so it is not counted at all. Only the Advanced paste-in path, through the counted link, adds to the number. Treat the Google Docs count as a floor: a fraction of the real installs.

So treat the combined total as a rough order-of-magnitude sense of reach, not a precise install tally.
