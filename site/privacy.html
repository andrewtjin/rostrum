Privacy

Rostrum counts anonymous downloads; and nothing else.

When you download manifest.xml from this site, a single number on our end goes up by one. That counter is the entire extent of what Rostrum collects.

What is collected

  • One anonymous number — a running total of how many times the manifest has been downloaded. It is not tied to you, your device, or anything you do afterward.

What is not collected

  • Nothing from your documents. Rostrum never sends, stores, or sees the text of anything you write or format.
  • No personal data. No names, emails, accounts, or sign-ins — there is no account to make.
  • No IP addresses or device fingerprints are retained by Rostrum. The counter records only that a download happened, never who or from where — we keep no request logs of our own. (The file is delivered through Cloudflare, which may process requests in transit under its own privacy policy.)
  • No tracking inside Word. The add-in itself sends nothing back as you use it — the count happens only at the moment you download the manifest file from this website.

Why

It tells us roughly how many people are using Rostrum over a season — enough to know the project is worth maintaining, and nothing more. The number is approximate: re-downloading the manifest to pick up a new version counts again too.
