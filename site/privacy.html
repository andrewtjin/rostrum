Privacy

Rostrum counts anonymous install clicks; and nothing else.

Rostrum ships on two platforms. The Word add-in installs from a manifest.xml download; Google Docs installs by clicking Make a copy of a template (with a manual Code.gs download as a fallback). Each time you click one of those install links on this site, the matching anonymous counter on our end goes up by one. Those counters (and their sum) are the entire extent of what Rostrum collects.

What is collected

  • Anonymous install counts: running totals of how many times the Word manifest.xml was downloaded and the Google Docs template was installed (the Make a copy link click, plus any Advanced Code.gs download). We also publish their sum (a rough cross-platform total). None of these is tied to you, your device, or anything you do afterward.

What is not collected

  • Nothing from your documents. Rostrum never sends, stores, or sees the text of anything you write or format, in Word or in Google Docs.
  • No personal data. No names, emails, accounts, or sign-ins. There is no account to make.
  • No IP addresses or device fingerprints are retained by Rostrum. The counters record only that an install link was clicked, never who or from where; we keep no request logs of our own. (Requests are served through Cloudflare, which may process them in transit under its own privacy policy.)
  • No tracking inside Word. The add-in itself sends nothing back as you use it. The count happens only at the moment you download manifest.xml from this website.
  • No tracking inside Google Docs. The Rostrum Code.gs only ever changes font size and style definitions; it sends nothing back as you use it: no usage pings, no document content. The only thing counted is the install click on this site — the Make a copy link (a redirect that tallies one anonymous integer, then sends you to Google's Copy dialog) or the Advanced Code.gs download. Nothing about the copy, or what you do in it, is ever seen.

Why

It tells us roughly how many people are using Rostrum over a season: enough to know the project is worth maintaining, and nothing more.

The number is deliberately approximate, and on both platforms it leans slightly high (it counts install clicks, some of which never finish):

  • Word over-counts a little: re-downloading manifest.xml to pick up a new version counts again.
  • Google Docs over-counts a little too: the Make a copy link counts the click, not a finished copy — clicking then cancelling Google's dialog, or re-copying to pick up a new version, each still counts. So, like Word, treat the Google Docs number as a rough upper-ish estimate, not a precise install tally.

So treat the combined total as a rough order-of-magnitude sense of reach, not a precise install tally.
